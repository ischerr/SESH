<p>Most renewable electricity generated in Scotland is from onshore wind.&nbsp; In&nbsp;<strong>2018</strong>, Scotland generated&nbsp;<strong>17,627 GWh</strong>&nbsp;via onshore wind,&nbsp;<strong>36.5%</strong>&nbsp;of all electricity generated in Scotland in 2018.&nbsp; This is the equivalent of powering&nbsp;<strong>4.5 million</strong>&nbsp;<strong>homes</strong>&nbsp;for a year.&nbsp; Onshore wind&nbsp;<strong>capacity</strong>&nbsp;is&nbsp;<strong>8.2 GW</strong>&nbsp;as of&nbsp;<strong>September 2019</strong>, with&nbsp;<strong>7.6 GW</strong>&nbsp;consented in the&nbsp;<strong>pipeline</strong>.&nbsp; Between&nbsp;<strong>2010</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>2018</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>capacity</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>generation</strong>&nbsp;of onshore wind in Scotland has more than&nbsp;<strong>tripled</strong>.&nbsp;&nbsp;Electricity generation via onshore wind directly supported approximately&nbsp;<strong>2,900&nbsp;</strong>full-time equivalent&nbsp;<strong>jobs&nbsp;</strong>in Scotland and generated&nbsp;<strong>&pound;2.08 billion&nbsp;</strong>in&nbsp;<strong>turnover&nbsp;</strong>in&nbsp;<strong>2018</strong>.</p>

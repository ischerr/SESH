<p><strong>Scotland</strong> generated almost <strong>26,500 GWh</strong> of <strong>renewable electricity</strong> in <strong>2018</strong>, the highest year to date. This is the equivalent of powering all domestic and non-domestic properties in Scotland for a year. Scotland&rsquo;s renewable electricity generation was<strong> 23.8%</strong> of the <strong>UK</strong>&rsquo;s in 2018.</p>
<p>Scotland is on track to <strong>exceed</strong> its 2018 renewable <strong>generation</strong> in <strong>2019</strong>; generation in the first nine months of 2019 is <strong>up 23%</strong> on the same period in 2018 (21,688 GWh).</p>
<p>Scotland generated the vast majority of its renewable electricity from <strong>wind</strong> (<strong>19,000 GWh</strong>), followed by <strong>hydro</strong> (<strong>5,000 GWh</strong>).</p>

<p>While&nbsp;<strong><u>solar</u>&nbsp;photovoltaics (PV) capacity&nbsp;</strong>in Scotland increased rapidly in the first half of this decade (<strong>2 MW&nbsp;</strong>in&nbsp;<strong>2010&nbsp;</strong>to&nbsp;<strong>326 MW&nbsp;</strong>in&nbsp;<strong>2016</strong>),&nbsp;<strong>progress&nbsp;</strong>has&nbsp;<strong>slowed&nbsp;</strong>in recent years, rising slightly to&nbsp;<strong>364 MW&nbsp;</strong>by&nbsp;<strong>September 2019</strong>.&nbsp; Solar PV generated&nbsp;<strong>329 GWh&nbsp;</strong>of electricity in&nbsp;<strong>2018</strong>, enough to power more than&nbsp;<strong>80,000 Scottish homes&nbsp;</strong>for a year.&nbsp; There is a further&nbsp;<strong>306 MW&nbsp;</strong>of solar projects in the pipeline as of September 2019.&nbsp;</p>

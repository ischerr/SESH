<p>Looking at the individual technologies for electricity generation in Scotland, <strong>nuclear (36.6%) </strong>and <strong>wind (34.8%) </strong>both make up more than one-third each of Scotland&rsquo;s domestic generation. <strong>Hydro </strong>makes up <strong>10.9% </strong>of Scotland&rsquo;s electricity generation.&nbsp;</p>
<p>By contrast, <strong>England and Wales </strong>use a significantly <strong>smaller proportion </strong>of <strong>low carbon (44.0%) </strong>and <strong>renewable (25.3%) </strong>sources for electricity generation, with <strong>gas </strong>contributing most to electricity generation <strong>45.5%</strong>).</p>

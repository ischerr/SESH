<p>Scotland <strong>generated</strong> almost <strong>26,500 GWh</strong> of <strong>renewable electricity</strong> in <strong>2018</strong>, the highest year to date. This is the equivalent of powering <strong>all domestic</strong> and <strong>non-domestic</strong> properties in Scotland for a <strong>year</strong>. Scotland&rsquo;s renewable electricity generation was <strong>23.8%</strong> of the <strong>UK</strong>&rsquo;s in 2018.</p>
<p>Scotland is on track to <strong>exceed</strong> its <strong>2018 generation</strong> in <strong>2019</strong>; generation in the first nine months of 2019 is <strong>up 23%</strong> on the same period in 2018 (21,688 GWh).</p>
<p>Scotland generated the vast majority of its renewable electricity from <strong>wind</strong> (<strong>19,000 GWh</strong>), followed by <strong>hydro</strong> (<strong>5,000 GWh</strong>).</p>
<p><strong>Wind</strong> generation in Scotland was a <strong>third</strong> of <strong>UK&rsquo;s</strong> wind generation in <strong>2018</strong>, and of EU countries, only Germany, Spain and France generated more electricity via wind in 2018.</p>
<p>Scotland&rsquo;s <strong>hydro</strong> generation made up <strong>91.6%</strong> of all hydro generation in the <strong>UK</strong> in <strong>2018</strong>, and twelve EU countries generated more electricity via hydro than Scotland in 2018.</p>

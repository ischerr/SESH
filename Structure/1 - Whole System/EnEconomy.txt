<p>Scotland&rsquo;s <strong>energy</strong> (including renewables) <strong>sector</strong> was identified in <a href="https://www.gov.scot/publications/scotlands-economic-strategy/">Scotland&rsquo;s Economic Strategy </a>as one of the growth sectors in which Scotland can build on existing comparative advantage and increase productivity and growth. The sector is defined using the <strong>Standard Industrialisation Classification (SIC) 2007</strong> codes that can be found <a href="https://www2.gov.scot/Resource/0054/00547085.docx">here.</a> Due to the sector definitions, the figures are not directly comparable with those on the low carbon and renewable sector or the oil and gas sector.</p>
<p>In <strong>2017</strong>, total <strong>turnover</strong> in the Scottish energy sector was <strong>&pound;43.8 billion, up 5.4%</strong> in nominal terms on <strong>2016</strong>. <strong>Extraction </strong>of<strong> crude petroleum </strong>and<strong> natural gas </strong>generated a third of this turnover <strong>(&pound;14.5 billion), </strong>and it<strong> increased </strong>by<strong> 16.1% </strong>from<strong> 2016 </strong>to<strong> 2017. GVA</strong> for the Energy growth sector totalled <strong>&pound;18.4 billion</strong> in <strong>2017, up 29.6%</strong> in nominal terms on <strong>2016</strong> to its highest level since 2014.</p>
<p>Total <strong>exports</strong> from the Energy growth sector stood at <strong>&pound;14 billion</strong> in <strong>2017</strong>, accounting for <strong>17.2%</strong> of <strong>Scotland&rsquo;s total exports</strong>. Exports from this sector <strong>increased</strong> by <strong>6.7%</strong> over the latest year.</p>
<p><strong>Employment</strong> in the energy sector stood at <strong>67,000 in 2018</strong>, down from <strong>69,000 </strong>in <strong>2017.</strong> <strong>Scotland</strong> accounted for <strong>20.2%</strong> of employment in Energy across <strong>Great Britain</strong> as a whole. Employment in the energy sector is highly <strong>concentrated</strong> in the <strong>North-East </strong>with<strong> 38.8%</strong> of all jobs in <strong>Aberdeen City</strong> and <strong>13.4%</strong> in <strong>Aberdeenshire.</strong></p>

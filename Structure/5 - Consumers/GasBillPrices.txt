<p>Like with electricity, average domestic gas prices <strong>increased </strong>in real terms compared to their <strong>lowest point </strong>in <strong>2001; direct debit </strong>customers&rsquo; bills almost <strong>doubled</strong>, <strong>standard credit</strong> customers&rsquo; bills increased by <strong>88.5%</strong> and <strong>prepayment</strong> customers by <strong>76.8%</strong>. The cost of gas rose at a faster rate than electricity. Scottish gas prices <strong>peaked</strong> in <strong>2014</strong>, and by <strong>2019</strong> <strong>dropped</strong> <strong>by</strong> approximately <strong>20%</strong> on this peak.</p>
<p><strong>Direct debit</strong> customers have the <strong>lowest</strong> bills in <strong>2019</strong> and <strong>standard credit</strong> the <strong>highest</strong>, <strong>7.4% more</strong>. Even though <strong>standard credit</strong> customers have the highest bills, they <strong>dropped</strong> by <strong>5.4%</strong> from <strong>2018</strong> and <strong>2019</strong>. In the same period, <strong>prepayment </strong>customers <strong>increased</strong> by <strong>6.4%</strong>.</p>

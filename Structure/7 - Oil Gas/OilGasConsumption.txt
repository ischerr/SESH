<p>Of the contribution oil and gas makes to primary energy in Scotland, just <strong>11.0% </strong>of it was <strong>consumed domestically </strong>in <strong>2017</strong>. This has dropped from <strong>13.3% </strong>in <strong>2012</strong>.</p>
<p>Even with the growth of renewables in recent years, oil and gas continue to make up the majority of Scotland&rsquo;s energy consumption. In 2017:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Oil and petroleum </strong>make up almost half (<strong>46.6%</strong>) of all Scottish consumption and <strong>gas </strong>makes up a third (<strong>32.2%</strong>). Just <strong>21.2% </strong>of all Scottish consumption comes from <strong>other fuels</strong>. This means that <strong>oil and gas </strong>make up <strong>78.8% </strong>of <strong>all Scottish consumption, </strong>slightly higher than <strong>75.3% </strong>for the <strong>UK </strong>as a whole<strong>.</strong></li>
<li>In the <strong>domestic </strong>sector in Scotland, <strong>gas </strong>makes up two-thirds of all consumption (<strong>67.0%</strong>).</li>
<li>Oil and gas make up <strong>almost all </strong>of Scotland&rsquo;s <strong>transport </strong>consumption, and <strong>91.3% </strong>of its <strong>heat </strong>demand.</li>
</ul>
